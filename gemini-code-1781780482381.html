The precast advantage

Built in a factory. Cured before it arrives.

Conventional RCC tanks need 21–28 days of on-site curing before they can hold water — time, weather, and labour risk the project carries every time. Our precast tanks are cast and cured under factory conditions, then delivered ready to commission. The schedule risk leaves the site before the tank does.

Lime Surfaces

The Vantage Store — Araish, Tadelakt, Marmorino & Venetian plaster for architects and designers

Most lime finishes sold in India are sold on look alone. We start from the chemistry — burn, slake, carbonation rate, breathability — because a finish that hasn't been tested for failure is a finish that fails on someone's wall, not in a lab. That rigor is the product as much as the texture is.

Why lime, done properly

The wall keeps curing after we leave.

Lime plaster doesn't just dry — it carbonates, slowly pulling CO₂ back out of the air to re-form limestone over weeks and months. Done right, that gives you a breathable, self-healing surface. Done without testing, it's the most common source of finish failure we see in the market. We document cure conditions, batch testing, and failure modes for every finish we ship.

  • Batch-tested slaking and carbonation rates
  • Documented cure schedules per finish & substrate
  • Failure-mode review before any large-format application
